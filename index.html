The State of Fashion

The fashion industry produces 92 million tons of textile waste per year. Everyone has seen this stat in a deck. Nobody has actioned it.

Overproduction runs at 30–40% of all units made. The rest are landfilled, incinerated, or described in press releases as "archived."

Gen Z wants vintage, secondhand, or nothing at all. This is very inconvenient for everyone and there is no plan.

The middle market is gone. Consumers are either buying luxury or nothing above €12. Leadership is aware. Leadership is in Lisbon.

These are serious, structural, multi-decade problems. A solution is clearly required. The room convenes. Someone orders artisan water.

NFTs are tried. Then the metaverse. 37 visitors over 14 months. One is a journalist writing about failure. A blockchain pilot tracks four jackets. One jacket is lost.

AI is announced as the new strategic priority. The problems remain. The press release does not mention them.

Fashion doesn't need to understand AI. It needs to be seen funding it. We are here to facilitate that at scale.
